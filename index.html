About Me

The Engineer Behind the Architecture

I didn't fall into software engineering — I was drawn to the intersection of logic and impact. Early in my career at Accenture, I learned that the best code isn't just clean; it's code that makes 10,000 users' lives measurably easier. That belief has shaped every architectural decision and every team conversation I've had since.

Over 8 years, I've progressed from building pension and insurance platforms to leading the full technical direction of enterprise research products at MarketCast — a company where I've now won the Best Performer Award three separate times. Along the way, I've developed a dual fluency that most engineers lean away from: I can architect a distributed Microservices system in the morning and translate its business implications to a non-technical stakeholder in the afternoon.

What drives me most right now is the convergence of AI and software architecture. I've seen firsthand how a well-integrated LLM can replace weeks of manual work overnight — and I've built systems that prove it. I'm looking for environments where ambitious technical problems meet real business stakes, and where engineering leadership means more than reviewing pull requests.
